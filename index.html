Academic Research Background

I graduated in 2023 with a PhD in Chemical Physics from the University of Maryland - College Park, where I conducted research on ultraintense short pulsed lasers, specifically investigating a method to measure the intensity at the focus of the laser by analyzing Thomson scattered light produced by the laser when it interacts with electrons ionized from a gas.

Presentations I have given:

  • two talks at the APS DAMOP Conference
  • a student seminar at the University of Maryland
  • candidacy talk
  • thesis defense

I have had the priviledge during my graduate research to work with a spectrometer with gated intensified CCD camera and a streak camera for measuring light at the nanosecond and sub-nanosecond time scale. When I was an undergraduate at UCLA, I developed graphics user interfaces (GUIs) using Python (PyQt, LabRAD) for computer control of scientific instruments through GPIB and serial ports.
